RMS-aware VAD

Conversion-time filter for VAD spans. Quiet bridges are removed before merge/pack, so they cannot glue separate speech islands into one large SHAR chunk. If the surviving spans are still close enough, merge works normally.

1. Raw VAD spans

VAD timestamps arrive as candidate speech spans. At this point they may include a low-energy bridge or pathological long span.

speech span kept quiet VAD bridge dropped over-limit span skipped before RMS decode final SHAR chunk

Without RMS-aware VAD

Small gaps around the quiet bridge can satisfy max_merge_gap_sec, so the bridge glues two speech regions into one chunk.

With RMS-aware VAD

The quiet bridge is removed before merge/pack, so the final chunks preserve the real speech islands.

Drop, then still merge

RMS drops the quiet micro-span first. The remaining speech gap is still within max_merge_gap_sec, so normal merge produces one chunk.
